Ghosts of Tsushima

"Honor died on the beach." - Jin Sakai

Story

The story of Ghost of Tsushima is one of the most beautifully painful stories ever told in any medium. Without getting into spoilers, you take the reins and play as Jin Sakai, a samurai of Tsushima Island and nephew to the island's lord, Shimura. The game takes place in 1274 during the first Mongol invasion of Japan, on the real island of Tsushima, a narrow stretch of land caught between mountainous forests, golden plains flowing in tandem with the wind, and scenic coastlines that put California and Florida to shame. You ride and fight as a samurai of the Samurai code, but when the old ways prove insufficient against the Mongol horde, Jin must ask himself a question that haunts the entire game: how far are you willing to go to save your people?

Characters

The characters in Ghosts of Tsushima also have a layer of depth, where Characters like Taka, with not a whole lot of screen time, have an emotional impact on Jin’s actions in the game, and the relationship between Jin and Lord Shimura, brings an emotional toll on Jin, Shimura, and the player. This relationship helps drive the game's story. Characters like Sensi Ishikawa, who have a long history with Jin, will let Jin help them with their personal battles, strengthening the bond between the player and the characters. If the player wants a more personal deep dive on Jin and his clan, the Iki Island DLC is also an amazing story and has a lot of great characters and allies.

Visuals/Gameplay

The gameplay is masterful. The few cons I can think of are the AI, which is either overly aggressive, to the point where like 3 or 4 enemies will attack you at once, making it very hard to parry, only leaving you the option to disengage. Or the AI is really passive, allowing you to take them one at a time. This can make encounters really difficult or really easy, which can break immersion, but I find that most fights are a good mix between aggressive and passive. When it comes to a sword combat game, I can’t name a game that does it better. The Stance system counters different enemy types and offers various combos. You can choose to be more of a bowman, and they are just as effective, each coming with different types of arrows. And you can choose to divert from the Samurai code and stick to the shadows, and strike enemies swiftly and silently. One mechanic I do like is that, instead of telling you exactly where something is, the wind guides the player in the right direction. I will end with a disclaimer: Just like Red Dead Redemption 2, Ghosts of Tsushima has its fast-paced combat moments, but many missions and storylines are slow burns. Instead of ignoring it, accept it and take in the visuals and the character dialogue. While there may not be over 300 different species in the game, finding a Haiku mat to reflect on Jin’s character and life may, in all actuality, be a reflection on your own.
