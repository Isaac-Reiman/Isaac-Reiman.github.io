Red Dead Redemption 2

"I gave you all I had." - Arthur Morgan

Story

The story of Red Dead Redemption 2 is my favorite story told by any media period. I really feel like Hollywood should take notes from this game on how to make an amazing story. Without getting into spoilers, you, as the player, take the reins and play as Arthur Morgan, one of the 3 original members of the Van Der Linde Gang. This game takes place in 1899 in a fictional area of the United States, somewhere in the Heartland, close to the southern red-dirt land and the dense forests of the north. Ride and live like an outlaw of the Wild West, making a name for yourself and the gang. With fortune comes misfortune, and the Van Der Lin Gang happens on some plans for a train heist, which would anger one man no one wants to anger. This is a big game, thus by proxy it will have a long story, but it is truly worth it in every way for the experience of being told the story.

Characters

The characters are all so well written, even characters that don’t have a lot of screen time. Characters like Kieran and Tilly. Characters seen most often, such as Arthur Morgan and Dutch Van Der Linde, are incredibly morally complex. When it comes to the characters, they are what make the game feel most alive, because you see the story's impressions left in them.

Visuals/Gameplay

The gameplay and the gameplay loop are amazing. Before we get into the pros lets start with the cons. One of the very few cons is that the horse's movement is bugged. When riding a horse, if you hit the pause menu then leave the menu until your horse comes to a complete stop, the inputs would be reversed: forward becomes backward, left becomes right, etc. If you are actively being searched for, you cannot start any missions, which is fine, but when the search stops, sometimes you still can’t start any missions until you restart the game. Granted, this has only happened to me a handful of times. Now, the pros: the core gameplay loop is amazing, with so many mission types that keep players engaged. But the oversimplified loop would be: New Act, then do all the main missions: a mission to find a new camp, clear the new camp, move everyone, and a New Act starts. Not to mention you don’t have to don’t to do nearly any missions, you can just go out into the wild of the game and discover over 300 unique species of animals. I will end on a disclaimer: Red Dead Redemption 2 is a slow-burn game. While there will be fast-paced gunfights, in slow-burning missions or areas, remember to take in the game's visuals and character dialogue.
